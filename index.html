40 years of computer experience

In 1985, my truck driver father had the brilliant idea "computers are going to be big one day" so he asked the secretary in his warehouse where they bought her computer. He took me to the IBM office in downtown Montreal on the Monday before xmas to buy me the first original IBM PC but he had no money left for software or games after that. I cried many nights, all my friends had Nintendos and Ataris. I split my paper route money with another nerd at my school to buy BASIC 1.0 so the only video games I played as a teenager were the ones I coded myself. In hindsight that act of tough love set me up on an amazing computer career at Corning, Disney, Mercedes-Benz, McLaren F1, BBC News, Environment Canada and many others.
